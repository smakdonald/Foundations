Theorem 8.21.

Let \((L, \preccurlyeq)\) be a lattice, and let \(x, y, z\in L\text{.}\)
  1. \(x \wedge x = x\) and \(x \vee x = x\) (Idempotent Laws).
  2. \(x \wedge y = y \wedge x\) and \(x \vee y = y \vee x\) (Commutative Laws).
  3. \(x \wedge (y \wedge z) = (x \wedge y) \wedge z\) and \(x \vee (y \vee z) = (x \vee y) \vee z\) (Associative Laws).
  4. \(x \wedge (x \vee y) = x\) and \(x \vee (x \wedge y) = x\) (Absorption Laws).
  5. \(x\preccurlyeq y\) if and only if \(x \wedge y = x\) if and only if \(x \vee y = y\text{.}\)
  6. If \(x\preccurlyeq y\text{,}\) then \(x \wedge z\preccurlyeq y \wedge z\) and \(x \vee z\preccurlyeq y \vee z\text{.}\)
