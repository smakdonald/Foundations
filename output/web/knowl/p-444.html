Let \(A\) and \(B\) be sets, let \(C, D \subseteq A\) and \(S,\sqcap \subseteq B\) be subsets, and let \(f : A \to B\) be a function. Let \(I\) and \(K\) be non-empty sets, let \({Ui}i\in I\) be a family of subsets of \(A\) indexed by \(I\text{,}\) and let \({Vk}k\in K\) be a family of subsets of \(B\) indexed by \(K\text{.}\)
  1. \(f ( \es ) = \es \) and \(f\inv ( \es ) = \es \text{.}\)
  2. \(f\inv (B) = A\text{.}\)
  3. \(f (C) \subseteq S\) if and only if \(C \subseteq f\inv (S)\text{.}\)
  4. If \(C \subseteq D\text{,}\) then \(f (C) \subseteq f (D)\text{.}\)
  5. If \(S \subseteq T\text{,}\) then \(f\inv (S) \subseteq f\inv (T )\text{.}\)
  6. \(f (\bigcup i\in I Ui) = \bigcup i\in I f (Ui)\text{.}\)
  7. \(f (⋂i\in I Ui) \subseteq ⋂i\in I f (Ui)\text{.}\)
  8. \(f\inv (\bigcup k\in K Vk) = \bigcup k\in K f\inv (Vk)\text{.}\)
  9. \(f\inv (⋂k\in K Vk) = ⋂k\in K f\inv (Vk)\text{.}\)
