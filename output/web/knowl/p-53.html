Let \(P, Q, R\) and \(S\) be statements.
  1. Modus Ponens.
    \(\displaystyle (P \to Q) \wedge P ⇒ Q\)
  2. Modus Tollens.
    \(\displaystyle (P \to Q) \wedge ¬Q ⇒ ¬P\)
  3. Simplification.
    \(\displaystyle P \wedge Q ⇒ P\)
  4. Simplification.
    \(\displaystyle P \wedge Q ⇒ Q\)
  5. Addition.
    \(\displaystyle P ⇒ P \vee Q\)
  6. Addition.
    \(\displaystyle Q ⇒ P \vee Q\)
  7. Modus Tollendo Ponens.
    \(\displaystyle (P \vee Q) \wedge ¬P ⇒ Q\)
  8. Modus Tollendo Ponens.
    \(\displaystyle (P \vee Q) \wedge ¬Q ⇒ P\)
  9. Biconditional-Conditional.
    \(\displaystyle P ↔ Q ⇒ P \to Q\)
  10. Biconditional-Conditional.
    \(\displaystyle P ↔ Q ⇒ Q \to P\)
  11. Conditional-Biconditional.
    \(\displaystyle (P \to Q) \wedge (Q \to P) ⇒ P ↔ Q\)
  12. Hypothetical Syllogism.
    \(\displaystyle (P \to Q) \wedge (Q \to R) ⇒ P \to R\)
  13. Constructive Dilemma.
    \(\displaystyle (P \to Q) \wedge (R \to S) \wedge (P \vee R) ⇒ Q \vee S\)
