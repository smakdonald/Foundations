Let \(I\) be a non-empty set, let \(\{A_i\}_{i\in I}\) be a family of sets indexed by \(I\) and let \(B\) be a set.
  1. \(\bigcap_{i\in I} A_i \subseteq A_k\) for all \(k\in I\text{.}\) If \(B \subseteq A_k\) for all \(k\in I\text{,}\) then \(B \subseteq\bigcap_{i\in I} A_i\) .
  2. \(A_k \subseteq\bigcup_{i\in I} A_i\) for all \(k\in I\text{.}\) If \(A_k \subseteq B\) for all \(k\in I\text{,}\) then \(\bigcup_{i\in I} A_i \subseteq B\text{.}\)
  3. Distributive Law.
    \(B \cap (\bigcup_{i\in I} A_i) = \bigcup_{i\in I}(B \cap A_i)\text{.}\)
  4. Distributive Law.
    \(B \cup (\bigcap_{i\in I} A_i) = \bigcap_{i\in I} (B \cup A_i)\text{.}\)
  5. De Morgan's Law.
    \(B \sm (\bigcup_{i\in I} A_i) = \bigcap_{i\in I} (B \sm A_i)\text{.}\)
  6. De Morgan's Law.
    \(B \sm (\bigcap_{i\in I} A_i) = \bigcup_{i\in I} (B \sm A_i)\text{.}\)
