Exploration 7.18.

  1. Give an example of sets \(A\text{,}\) \(B\) and \(C\) such that \(A \# B\) and \(A \cup C \not\# B \cup C\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(A\text{,}\) \(B\) and \(C\) be sets. Suppose that \(A \# B\) and that \(A ∩C = \es\) and \(B ∩C = \es\) . Prove that \(A \cup C \# B \cup C\text{.}\)
  3. Let \(A\text{,}\) \(B\) and \(C\) be sets. Suppose that \(A \cup C \# B \cup C\) and that \(A ∩C = \es\) and \(B ∩ C = \es\) . Is it necessarily the case that \(A \# B\text{?}\) Give a proof or a counterexample.
