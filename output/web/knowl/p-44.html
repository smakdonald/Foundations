The statement “Irene has red hair and she does not have red hair” is a contradiction. In symbols this statement is \(P \wedge ¬P\text{,}\) and it has truth table The statement \(P \wedge ¬P\) is always false, regardless of whether \(P\) is true or false. In general, a statement is a contradiction if, as verified using a truth table, it is always false, regardless of whether its component statements are true or false.
