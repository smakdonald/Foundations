Theorem 3.25.

Let \(A\text{,}\) \(B\text{,}\) \(C\) and \(D\) be sets.
  1. If \(A \subseteq B\) and \(C \subseteq D\text{,}\) then \(A \times C \subseteq B \times D\text{.}\)
  2. \(A \times (B \cup C) = (A \times B) \cup (A \times C)\) and \((B \cup C) \times A = (B \times A) \cup (C \times A)\) (Distributive Laws).
  3. \(A \times (B \cap C) = (A \times B) \cap (A \times C)\) and \((B \cap C) \times A = (B \times A) \cap (C \times A)\) (Distributive Laws).
  4. \(A \times\emptyset = \emptyset\) and \(\emptyset \times A = \emptyset\text{.}\)
  5. \((A \cap B) \times (C \cap D) = (A \times C) \cap (B \times D)\text{.}\)
