Certainly, two different English sentences can convey equivalent statements, for example “if it rains I will stay home” and “I will stay home if it rains.” These two statements are both English variants of \(P → Q\text{,}\) where \(P = \)“it rains,” and \(Q = \)“I will stay home.” The difference between these two statements is an issue only of the flexibility of the English language; symbolically, these two statements are identical, not just equivalent.
