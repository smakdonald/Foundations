  1. Suppose that \(f\) is a meet homomorphism. Let \(\wedge and \wedge′\) denote the meet for \(L\) and \(M\text{,}\) respectively. Let \(x, y\in L\text{.}\) Suppose that \(x\preccurlyeq y\text{.}\) Then by Theorem 7.5.3 (6) we know that \(x = x \wedge y\text{.}\) Then \(f (x) = f (x \wedge y) = f (x) \wedge ′ f (y)\text{,}\) because \(f\) is a meet homomorphism. Using Theorem 7.5.3 (6) again, we deduce that \(f (x) \preccurlyeq′ f (y)\text{.}\) It follows that \(f\) is an order homomorphism. A similar argument works if \(f\) is a join homomorphism; we omit the details.
