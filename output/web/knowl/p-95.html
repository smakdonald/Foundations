Let \(P\text{,}\) \(Q\) and \(R\) be statements.
  1. Commutative Law.
    \(P ∨ Q ⇔ Q ∨ P\text{.}\)
  2. Commutative Law.
    \(P \wedge Q ⇔ Q \wedge P\text{.}\)
  3. Associative Law.
    \((P ∨ Q) ∨ R ⇔ P ∨ (Q ∨ R)\text{.}\)
  4. Associative Law.
    \((P \wedge Q) \wedge R ⇔ P \wedge (Q \wedge R)\text{.}\)
  5. Distributive Law.
    \(P \wedge (Q ∨ R) ⇔ (P \wedge Q) ∨ (P \wedge R\text{.}\)
  6. Distributive Law.
    \(P ∨ (Q \wedge R) ⇔ (P ∨ Q) \wedge (P ∨ R)\text{.}\)
  7. \(P \to Q ⇔ ¬P ∨ Q\text{.}\)
  8. Contrapositive.
    \(P \to Q ⇔ ¬Q \to ¬P\text{.}\)
  9. \(P ↔ Q ⇔ Q ↔ P\text{.}\)
  10. \(P ↔ Q ⇔ (P \to Q) \wedge (Q \to P)\text{.}\)
  11. De Morgan’s Law.
    \(¬(P \wedge Q) ⇔ ¬P ∨ ¬Q\text{.}\)
  12. De Morgan’s Law.
    \(¬(P ∨ Q) ⇔ ¬P \wedge ¬Q\text{.}\)
  13. \(¬(P \to Q) ⇔ P \wedge ¬Q\text{.}\)
  14. \(¬(P ↔ Q) ⇔ (P \wedge ¬Q) ∨ (¬P \wedge Q)\text{.}\)
