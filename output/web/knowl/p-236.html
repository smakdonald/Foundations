There are two commonly used strategies, each one being advantageous in certain situations. One approach would be to use the contrapositive together with De Morgan’s Law (Fact 1.3.2 (13)), which together imply that P → (A∨B) is equivalent to (¬A∧¬B) → ¬P. The other would be to use Exercise 1.3.2 (5), which says that P → (A ∨ B) is equivalent to (P ∧ ¬A) → B. The roles of A and B could also be interchanged in this last statement.
