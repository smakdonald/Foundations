Let \(P\) and \(Q\) be statements. The biconditional from \(P\) to \(Q\text{,}\) which is denoted \(P ↔ Q\text{,}\) is the statement that, intuitively, is true if \(P\) and \(Q\) are both true or both false, and is false otherwise.
