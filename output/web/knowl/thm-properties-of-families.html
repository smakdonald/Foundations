Theorem 3.32.

Let \(I\) be a non-empty set, let \(\{A_i\}i\in I\) be a family of sets indexed by \(I\) and let \(B\) be a set.
  1. \(\bigcap i\in I A_i \subseteq A k\) for all \(k\in I\text{.}\) If \(B \subseteq A k\) for all \(k\in I\text{,}\) then \(B \subseteq\bigcap i\in I A_i\) .
  2. \(Ak \subseteq\bigcup i\in I A_i\) for all \(k\in I\text{.}\) If \(A k \subseteq B\) for all \(k\in I\text{,}\) then \(\bigcup i\in I A_i \subseteq B\text{.}\)
  3. Distributive Law.
    \(B \cap (\bigcup i\in I A_i) = \bigcup i\in I (B \cap A_i)\text{.}\)
  4. Distributive Law.
    \(B \cup (\bigcap i\in I A_i) = \bigcap i\in I (B \cup A_i)\text{.}\)
  5. De Morgan's Law.
    \(B - (\bigcup i\in I A_i) = \bigcap i\in I (B - A_i)\text{.}\)
  6. De Morgan's Law.
    \(B - (\bigcap i\in I A_i) = \bigcup i\in I (B - A_i)\text{.}\)
