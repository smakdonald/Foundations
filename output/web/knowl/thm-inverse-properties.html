Theorem 4.12.

Let \(A\) and \(B\) be sets, and let \(f : A \to B\) be a function.
  1. If \(f\) has an inverse, then the inverse is unique.
  2. If \(f\) has a right inverse \(g\) and a left inverse \(h\text{,}\) then \(g = h\text{,}\) and hence \(f\) has an inverse.
  3. If \(g\) is an inverse of \(f\text{,}\) then \(f\) is an inverse of \(g\text{.}\)
