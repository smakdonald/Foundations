  1. Suppose that \(n\) and \(m\) are both even. Then there exist integers \(k\) and \(j\) such that \(n = 2k\) and \(m = 2 j\text{.}\) Then
    \begin{equation*} n + m = 2k + 2 j = 2(k + j). \end{equation*}
    Because \(k\) and \(j\) are integers, so is \(k + j\text{.}\) Hence \(m + n\) is even.
